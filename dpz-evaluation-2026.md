## PHASE 3: BUFFETT FRAMEWORK

### SECTION 1: BUSINESS QUALITY

#### 1.1 Simple & Understandable ✅

**Business Model:**  
Domino's is a franchise royalty business. Franchisees pay upfront fees + 5.5% ongoing royalties. Domino's also operates a captive supply chain, selling dough/cheese/toppings to franchisees for additional margin. Revenue scales as store count grows globally.

**Rating:** 9/10 (PASS)  
**Buffett verdict:** Simple and in his wheelhouse

#### 1.2 Durable Moat ✅

**Moat Type:** FRANCHISE NETWORK + BRAND + DIGITAL PLATFORM

**Evidence:**
- 20,600+ stores in 90+ countries (scale advantage)
- #1 pizza delivery globally
- 75%+ orders via app (digital leader)
- Captive supply chain = recurring revenue
- "Fortressing" strategy (densely cluster stores for speed)

**Risks:**
- Third-party delivery (Uber Eats, DoorDash) bypassing direct relationship
- Ghost kitchens lowering barriers to entry
- Franchisee financial stress (bankruptcies reported 2025-2026)

**Rating:** 8/10 (PASS)  
**Moat:** Wide but under pressure

#### 1.3 Consistent Operating History ✅

**Profitability:** 15+ consecutive years profitable  
**EPS growth:** 23% CAGR (exceptional)  
**Disruptions:** Only COVID 2020 (-15% EPS)

**Rating:** 10/10 (PASS)  
**Buffett verdict:** Textbook consistency

#### 1.4 Favorable Long-Term Prospects ⚠️

**Industry:** Global QSR growing 4-6% annually  
**Tailwinds:** Delivery culture, digital ordering  
**Headwinds:** Labor costs, food inflation, competition  

**20-year outlook:** Likely exists but faces margin pressure

**Rating:** 7/10 (PASS)  
**Buffett verdict:** Good but not great

### SECTION 6: FINAL VERDICT

**Score:** 98/130 (75%)

**Buffett Would:** AVOID at current price

**Summary:**

Domino's is an exceptional franchise business with a capital-light model, wide moat, and 15+ years of consistent profitability (23% EPS CAGR). The business quality is outstanding: 34% ROA, 12% net margins, minimal CapEx, and a proven digital-first strategy. However, the company is significantly OVERVALUED at $377.79. Intrinsic value is $278/share (base case), implying a -36% margin of safety. The aggressive debt-funded buyback strategy ($5B net debt, 4.7x EBITDA) creates financial risk, especially with franchisee bankruptcies emerging. Buffett would love the business but PASS at this price. Wait for a 30%+ pullback.

**Biggest Risk:**

Franchisee financial collapse triggers store closures and brand damage. If labor costs, food inflation, and third-party delivery competition continue squeezing franchisee margins, Domino's faces: (1) Slower store growth (hurts royalty revenue), (2) Supply chain volume decline (hurts margin), (3) Brand inconsistency from closures. With $5B debt, financial flexibility is limited if crisis accelerates. FCF could drop 40% in severe downturn, making debt unsustainable.

**Comparable Buffett Holdings:**

McDonald's (MCD) - both are franchise royalty businesses with global scale, brand moats, and capital-light models. Key difference: McDonald's has stronger brand equity, lower leverage (2x EBITDA vs 4.7x), and trades at similar valuation multiples. Domino's offers higher growth (6% vs 4%) but higher risk. Buffett owns McDonald's and would prefer it for balance sheet safety.
