### 2. Does the business have a durable competitive moat?

**Analysis based on SEC data:**
- Review moat description from 10-K (in qualitativeData.moat)
- Check competitive advantages mentioned in Item 1
- Examine Risk Factors (Item 1A) for competitive threats
- Assess pricing power from margin stability (financialData.margins)

**Moat types (identify from SEC filings):**
- Brand (pricing power, customer loyalty mentioned in 10-K)
- Network effects (user growth, platform effects in 10-K)
- Switching costs (retention data, contract terms in 10-K)
- Cost advantage (economies of scale, manufacturing efficiency in 10-K)
- Efficient scale (limited competition mentioned in Risk Factors)

**Evidence from SEC data:**
- Margin trends (rising margins = pricing power)
- Market share (from 10-K business description if disclosed)
- Customer retention (from MD&A if disclosed)
- Competitive position (from Risk Factors)

**Score:** 0-10 points
- 9-10: Wide moat with multiple sources, clear from 10-K
- 7-8: Wide moat but single source or emerging threats noted in Risk Factors
- 5-6: Narrow moat, some advantages but not dominant
- 3-4: Weak moat, significant competitive threats in Risk Factors
- 0-2: No moat, commoditized business per 10-K

---

### 3. Does the business have consistent operating history?

**Analysis based on SEC data:**
- Check financialData.eps for profitability every year
- Calculate EPS CAGR from historical data
- Look for disruptions, losses, or restructurings

**Metrics to check:**
```javascript
// From financialData.eps
const epsHistory = data.financialData.eps.rows;
const downYears = epsHistory.filter(row => row[2].startsWith('-')).length;
const totalYears = epsHistory.length;

// From financialData.revenue
const revenueGrowth = data.financialData.revenue.rows;
const negativeGrowthYears = revenueGrowth.filter(row => row[2].startsWith('-')).length;
```

**Questions:**
- Profitable every year for last 10 years?
- EPS grew consistently (CAGR >0)?
- Revenue grew consistently?
- Any bankruptcies, major restructurings, or losses mentioned in 10-K?

**Score:** 0-10 points
- 10: Perfect record (10+ years profit, consistent EPS growth)
- 8-9: Excellent (1 down year due to external shock like COVID)
- 6-7: Good (2-3 down years, but generally growing)
- 4-5: Inconsistent (erratic growth, multiple down years)
- 0-3: Poor (losses, bankruptcies, major disruptions)

---

### 4. Does the business have favorable long-term prospects?

**Analysis based on SEC data:**
- Review Industry Analysis from 10-K (in qualitativeData.industry)
- Check Risk Factors (Item 1A) for long-term threats
- Assess regulatory environment from Risk Factors
- Consider disruption risks mentioned in 10-K

**Questions:**
- Is the industry growing per 10-K MD&A?
- Will this business likely exist in 20 years?
- What are biggest long-term threats per Risk Factors?
- Are tailwinds stronger than headwinds per management discussion?

**Score:** 0-10 points
- 9-10: Strong long-term prospects, growing industry, minimal disruption risk
- 7-8: Good prospects, some headwinds but manageable
- 5-6: Uncertain, balanced tailwinds and headwinds
- 3-4: Weak prospects, declining industry or major disruption risk
- 0-2: Poor prospects, existential threats in Risk Factors
